Lucca - a Talia demo application

Welcome to Talia. This demo contains a sample library with some information about the city of Lucca. Of course there's still a lot missing, but you can already take a first look.

This is the page about Lucca. Lucca is a city in Tuscany, northern central Italy, situated on the river Serchio in a fertile plain near (but not on) the Tyrrhenian Sea. It is the capital city of the Province of Lucca. Among other reasons, it is famous for its intact Renaissance-era city walls (although the city has expanded beyond the wall's boundaries).

What can you do here?

On the left of your screen you will see the sidebar. This area contains the tools to navigate the database. You can collapse the sidebar by clicking the arrow button. Clicking again will restore it.

Currently the sidebar has two navigation mechanisms: When you view a Source, it will show the content related to it. Or you can click the "house" button to navigate through the ontology of our demo - showing the types of elements contained in the database.

When you click the "document" icon, you will go back to viewing Sources.

On top of this text, to the right, you will see the toolbar. In the final version of Talia, this will contain tools to work with the Source that you are looking at.

At the moment it just contains two example buttons, one of which will take you back to this start page.
